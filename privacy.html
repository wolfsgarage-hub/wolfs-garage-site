PRIVACY POLICY

Wolf’s Garage is a community hub, not a data operation. Here’s exactly what we collect, why, and what we’ll never do with it. Plain English, because that’s how we talk.

Last updated: July 22, 2026 · Applies to wolfsgarage.com and its /events and /directory pages

// The short version

We collect what you hand us and nothing sneaky. An account needs a nickname, email, and password. Posting photos means we store your photos. Submitting an event means we store the event.

We don’t sell your information. We don’t run ad trackers. No Facebook pixel, no Google Analytics, no data brokers. Our only analytics is a first-party counter that records button names, never who clicked.

The newsletter is opt-in. A checkbox you have to check yourself. Your account works fine without it.

// What we collect, and why

  • Account info — nickname, email address, and password when you join the Pack. The password goes to our sign-in provider (Firebase, run by Google) in encrypted form; we never see or store it ourselves. This exists so your posts and photos belong to you.
  • Stuff you post — photos you upload to the Pack Gallery, bulletin board posts, comments, and hearts. That’s the point of the site: you post it so people can see it. Wolf reviews submissions before they go public.
  • Event submissions — event details, an optional flyer image, and your email address if you include it so we can follow up about your event. Event listings are public; your email never is.
  • Business listings — if you list your business in the Hot Rodder Directory, the listing info you give us is public on purpose. That’s what a directory is.
  • Newsletter signup — your email, only if you check the box or use the signup form. One or two emails a month. Unsubscribe link in every one.
  • Boring server stuff — our hosting provider (Vercel) keeps standard server logs, like every website on earth. We use them to keep the site running, not to profile you.

// What we will never do

  • Sell, rent, or trade your information. To anyone. Ever.
  • Run third-party ad trackers or advertising pixels.
  • Auto-enroll you in the mailing list. (We used to add new accounts automatically. We stopped. If you signed up back then and don’t want it, hit unsubscribe — it works.)
  • Ask for information a car community has no business asking for.

// Analytics, honestly

When you click certain buttons — like “Find an Event” — the site sends us the name of the button and nothing else. No email, no location, no search terms, no form contents, no ID tying clicks together. It tells us “12 people clicked Find an Event today,” not who they were. That’s the whole system.

// Who holds what

We’re a small operation, so a few established services store data on our behalf. Each one only gets what it needs to do its job:

Firebase (Google)Account sign-in (email + encrypted password) and community content: gallery photos, bulletin posts, comments, hearts.
CloudinaryHosts uploaded images — gallery photos and event flyers.
SupabaseThe event listings database behind the Event Finder.
MailchimpThe newsletter list. Only emails that opted in.
VercelHosts the site; keeps standard server logs.
ShopifyRuns the merch shop at shop.wolfsgarage.com. Purchases happen there under Shopify’s privacy policy — we see order info, never your card number.
Google Fonts / MapsThe site loads fonts from Google, and the Directory map is Google Maps. Google receives the standard technical data any web request includes.

No other third parties. If that list ever grows, this page changes first.

// Your choices

  • Unsubscribe — link at the bottom of every newsletter. Instant, no guilt trip.
  • Delete your stuff — want a photo, post, comment, listing, or your whole account gone? Email us and it’s gone: wolfsgaragenw@gmail.com.
  • See your data — ask at the same address and we’ll tell you exactly what we have on you. It won’t take long. There isn’t much.

// The fine print, still in English

This site isn’t aimed at kids under 13, and we don’t knowingly collect their information. Wolf’s Garage LLC is based in Portland, Oregon, USA, and your data is handled in the United States. If we ever change how any of this works, this page gets updated and the date up top moves.

Questions? wolfsgaragenw@gmail.com. A person reads it. Usually the same person who built the site you’re on.
